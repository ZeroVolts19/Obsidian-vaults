### Injury classifications:
	- Permanent injury / Death:
		- death.
		- amputation.
		- loss of a sense.
	- Medical treatment:
		- requires treatment from a trained medical professional, or prescrption medication.
	- First aid:
		- administered immediately after injury occurs, and at the location where it occurred. requires little training or technology to administer.
### Hazards - Terms & Definitions
**Hazard** - the potential for harm, including characteristics of things, actions, or people.
	- **probability of Occurence** - how likely it is the person will encounter the hazard. **Must consider each person's activities** and how that relates to the identified hazard.
	- **Controlling a Hazard** - **using safeguarding means** to prevent a person from encountering a Hazard or limiting a persons access to the Hazard Zone. 
	- **Exposure to Hazard** - **How often the person is exposed** to the hazard.
	- **Hazard Zone** - Any **area** in or **near** a **machine** or piece of equipment, which **has** the **potential to cause injury.**
	- **Safeguarding** - **Controlling a hazard** by some **method like** a **physical barrier** **or** an **electronic interlocking guard.** 
### Risk Assessment
injuries often result from failure to identify machine hazards.
	- the target is for engineers to design inherently safe machines. 
	- Machine hazard examples:
		- Mechanical, crush, draw-in, nip/pinch, impact, laceration, puncture.
		- electrical, shock, arch flash, arc blast, static shocks, electrocution. 
		- Thermal, skin burns, eyes.
		- Noise.
		- Vibration.
		- Radiation, X-rays, ultra-violet.
		- Chemicals, long term health effects ex: cancer, breathing difficulty,  short term health effects ex: dizziness.
		- Ergonomics, long term back injury, arthritis,
		- Falling / Ejected, Robot dropped parts, air ejection, fluid ejection/injection
		- Loass of Stabilityg
		- Slip, trip, fall
### RA steps overview
#### Step 1. Determination of limits
- Use limits:
	- Modes.
	- Compatible materials.
	- Required operator experience.
	- Noise Exposure.
- Space limits:
	- range of machine movements
	- space needed to clean and maintain
- Time limits:
	- Machine Lifespan, wearable components, T10d safety components.
- Other limits:
	- Temperature.
	- Humidity.
	- Dust, Class I, II, Div I, II.
	- Elevation (component compatibility).
#### Step 2. Hazard Identification
- It is useful and required to consider the potential hazards of a machine by considering who will interact with the machine as well as the machine aspects.
##### Potential Groups:
- Machine Operator (O).
- Maintenance (M).
- Other (C).
##### Potential Tasks:
- Packing & Transport.
- Unloading / Unpacking.
- Systems Installation.
- Startup, Commissioning & Decommissioning.
- Set Up and Try-out.
- Operation ( all modes).
- Tool Change.
- Planned & Unplanned Maintenance.
- Major Repair & Troubleshooting.
- Recovery from Crash.
- Housekeeping & Disposal.

#### Step 3. Risk Estimation
- to estimate the risk for any identified hazard, consider **Severity**, **Exposure**, and **Probability of Occurrence** (**of Harm**).

###### Severity (of Harm): 
- rarely changes during the course of a risk assessment (unless still in design phase). Due to Severity being reduced by either elimination,  substitution, or PPE. 
###### Frequency of Exposure:
Consider factors like:
- Purpose of access to hazard zone.
- Time spent in danger zone.
- Number of operators or personnel accessing the machine and the frequency of that access.
