
## Please briefly elaborate on your experience in equipment specification planning and implementation for capital projects.*

I have been responsible for implementing capital projects since the beginning of my engineering career. 

Co-Op Engineer, 90% safety remediation project - During my Co-Op at Mallinckrodt, I was responsible for remediating a safety issue on a newly qualified piece of equipment. This was my baptism into functional safety; I worked under a MS EE, who was well-versed in functional safety. I was invited to apply for a fulltime Jr. Automation engineer position and then advanced to Automation engineer.
 
Automation Engineer, 50% capital projects/50% production support - Working for Mallinckrodt pharmaceuticals, I completed several 100% electrical, controls, functional safety update projects for aging equipment. In my experience in pharma, the mechanical elements of the equipment often have a 30yr+ service life, while the controls are dutifully replaced every 10 years for obsolescence and cGMP rationales. I was responsible for designing the updated electrical systems and writing the new PLC programs and HMI runtimes;  this was because the hardware and software platforms would be completely incompatible and obsolete after 10yrs. One element of these updates were always to upgrade the safety-related parts of the control system (SRP/CS) to the state of the art technology and adherence to up to date functional safety standards. 

Automation Engineer, 100% capital projects - Working for Corning Inc, I was part of a group that functioned as an internal integrator for the company, where all the specification came from internal sources. I served as the electrical and functional safety lead on new automation design projects and a functional safety SME for . I had a very high percentage of the technical ownership for the electrical design and functional safety design.

## Please briefly elaborate on your experience with Controls Reliability and Safety Circuit Analysis.*

Following that, I joined an internal integrator group where I was the Electrical and FS lead on each capital project I was assigned. I was the functional safety SME for my dept., plant, and I also would regularly consult on functional safety designs, MRAs, by various other design groups within Corning all over NA. I also defined standards for my business division, optical connectivity (fiber connectorization etc.).
